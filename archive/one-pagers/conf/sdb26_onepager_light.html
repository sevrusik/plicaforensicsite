Open benchmark for measuring document fraud resilience — not vendor marketing accuracy. Most KYC accuracy claims use undisclosed corpora and unknown generator versions. SDB-26 defines what to measure, how to measure it, and how to report it — reproducibly and comparably across stacks and vendors.

The Problem
  • KYC accuracy claims use undisclosed corpora — results are not comparable
  • No standard measure of bypass risk across generators and attack levels
  • Agent-mediated onboarding increases attack throughput without changing detection
  • Synthetic document quality improves 4× faster than verification stacks update
  • "Works in demo" becomes "fails in production" without measurable baselines
What SDB-26 Provides
  • Public measurement framework for synthetic document risk
  • Standardized attack levels (L1 / L2 / L3)
  • Reproducible reporting format with machine-validatable JSON schema
  • Explainable output layer via Forensic Reason Codes (FRC)
  • FRC A2A Extension for agentic onboarding contexts
  • Open corpus methodology — independently verifiable
Attack Levels
L1 Standard Generation
Direct API / Web Output
DALL-E 3, Midjourney v7, Stable Diffusion XL, Flux 2, ChatGPT Image 2, Kling O1. Direct generation output.
L2 Advanced Diffusion
Specialised Pipelines
LoRA fine-tuned on document datasets. Targeted manipulation of genuine documents. Metadata-aware generation.
L3 Screenshot Attack
Screen Recapture
Document displayed on screen (FHD / 2K / 4K) and photographed. Produces genuine camera EXIF. Moiré-dependent detection.
What Is Measured
BR
Bypass Rate
% of synthetic documents incorrectly trusted by the stack.
CG
Confidence Gap
Mean confidence on incorrect approvals. Measures calibration.
GS
Generator Sensitivity
BR stratified by generator family. Shows which models bypass.
FPR
False Positive Rate
Impact on genuine users. Required companion to BR.
Why It Matters Now
  • Synthetic quality improving 4× faster than detection update cycles
  • Anthropic and OpenAI now ship production KYC agent templates
  • Agent-submitted documents require a separate forensic layer (FRC A2A)
  • Regulators moving from feature lists to effectiveness evidence
  • Enterprise buyers requiring independent bypass measurement
Typical Outcomes
  • Comparable baseline across vendors on the same corpus
  • Clear failure points by attack type and generator family
  • Measurable improvement roadmap instead of subjective tuning
  • Audit-ready FRC output for regulatory and legal review
  • Schema-valid results publishable to the open SDB-26 registry
