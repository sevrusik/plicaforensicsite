Physics-first document verification for regulated onboarding workflows. Content checks prove that a document looks correct. DocLens proves that a document is physically real — by analysing the signal layer beneath the content, where synthetic artifacts always leave a trace.

The Problem
  • Content checks alone do not prove authenticity — they verify plausibility
  • Synthetic, edited, or screen-recaptured artifacts can pass policy checks
  • AI-generated documents bypass visual classifiers trained on last year's models
  • Analysts spend time on manual triage with weak forensic context
  • No structured audit trail when escalation is challenged post-incident
What DocLens Does
  • Analyses document authenticity using layered forensic signals
  • Physics-level examination — not pattern matching against known fakes
  • Produces structured reason codes — human and machine readable
  • Generates audit-ready decision artifacts for compliance operations
  • Integrates into existing review queues without workflow disruption
Forensic Signal Layers
L0
Provenance
