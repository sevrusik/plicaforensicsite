Forensic Reason Codes · calibrated risk · audit trail
Show the physics of the file — not a lone probability.

Most stacks stop at a score. Stratum separates signal into strata — metadata and provenance, intrinsic pixel physics, model-based generation cues, and policy-aware aggregation — then emits structured Forensic Reason Codes (FRC) and confidence that is explicitly calibrated (raw vs. reportable), so compliance and ops can defend the decision without trusting a black box.

The Stratum stack — signal layers
L1
PROVENANCE
Provenance layer
Metadata validation, consistency checks, and content-credentials style signals where present. Establishes what the file claims about its own origin before pixels are interpreted.
EXIF · C2PA / credentials
L2
PHYSICS
Intrinsic layer
Pixel-level integrity: compression artifacts (e.g. JPEG quantization), noise and frequency cues, diffusion / GAN-style fingerprints, ELA-style tamper hints — the “geology” of the image.
Quant · FFT · PRNU-style noise
L3
MODEL
Model layer
AI-generation and editing detectors with explicit confidence; combined with intrinsic cross-checks so high model confidence cannot bypass physics-layer spoofing tests.
Ensemble · guardrails
L4
AGGREGATE
Aggregation layer
Fraud scoring with rule-based floors for strong intrinsic combinations, verdict-aware calibration, and logging that records why raw confidence was adjusted for the report.
FRC mapping · calibration notes
L5
PACKET
Evidence packet
API and report outputs structured for review: per-detector breakdown, timings, reason codes, and export paths (e.g. JSON + PDF) suitable for pilot KPIs and manual QA.
Case packet · stage timing
Verdict posture
CONSISTENT
Layers agree; low forensic tension. Typical “real” posture when physics and provenance align.
UNCERTAIN
Mixed or weak signals — calibrated confidence reflects ambiguity; human review or resubmit.
HIGH RISK
Strong model and/or intrinsic evidence; floors and FRCs document the basis for action.
Why it matters now
  • Agent-mediated flows add a new spoofing surface: provenance claims vs. pixel reality
  • Regulators and partners increasingly ask for effectiveness evidence, not only process
  • Independent benchmarks (SDB-26) make generator drift and bypass rates discussable
  • Boutique depth: methodology, disclosure, and pilot metrics — not a generic risk API
