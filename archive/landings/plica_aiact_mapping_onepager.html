Conference-Ready Message
In AI Act high-risk contexts, the argument is no longer "we had human review." The argument is "we can prove which object was evaluated, what changed, and why the decision path is reproducible."
Regulatory Signals Relevant to Plica
Intended purpose drives classification
Marketing, docs, terms, and technical design must be coherent. Human involvement alone does not remove high-risk classification.
Split architectures treated as whole
Splitting workflows into modules does not avoid classification if combined outputs materially influence decisions.
Annex III Point 5 — finance
Creditworthiness/credit score: high-risk. Life/health insurance pricing: high-risk. Fraud detection carve-out exists — but mixed-purpose stacks need careful purpose boundaries.
Article 6(3) filter is narrow
Exceptions are conditional and monitored. Profiling in covered contexts is generally not filter-eligible. Cannot be treated as default escape.
Direction is clear, binding later
Final interpretation remains with CJEU. But organisations should already assume stricter documentation and traceability expectations.

Positioning
Plica is not a replacement for KYC or credit models. It is the object-level evidence layer beneath them — making classification, compliance, and audit positions defensible.
