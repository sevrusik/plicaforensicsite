The problem

Three things that break
before your detector runs.

01 ·
The pipeline rewrites the file

Resize, JPEG recompress, format convert, antivirus sanitise, S3 optimise. Each operation changes the forensic evidence in the file — without logging what changed. The detector evaluates a derivative, not the original.

FRC-L-RESIZE-DETECTED · FRC-L-DOUBLE-COMPRESSION
02 ·
The audit trail is broken at intake

SHA-256 at decision ≠ SHA-256 at intake. The file your detector examined is not the file your client submitted. When a regulator asks for the original, it no longer exists in verifiable form.

FRC-L-HASH-CHAIN-BROKEN · FRC-L-PIPELINE-TRANSFORM-UNDOCUMENTED
03 ·
Vendor drift goes unmeasured

Your KYC vendor updated their model. You received an email. Nobody measured what changed in your false positive rate and bypass rate — before and after. Drift is invisible until it's a regulatory finding.

The methodology

P · L · I · C · A

P
Provenance

Where did this file come from? Does its digital biography match what was claimed? Metadata sterility, temporal consistency, channel attestation.

L
Lineage

What happened to this file inside your own pipeline? Every resize, recompress, format conversion — logged as a structured transform event with before/after hash.

I
Integrity

How much physical signal survived? PRNU sensor noise, DCT coefficient statistics, FFT frequency artifacts, ELA zone analysis. The signals that distinguish a camera from a generator.

C
Consistency

Are signals from different layers logically compatible? Metadata claims vs. physical sensor signals vs. compression history. Regional forgery leaves inconsistencies global scores miss.

A
Authenticity

Final compound verdict: GENUINE · SYNTHETIC · EDITED · SCREENSHOT · INSUFFICIENT. Schema-valid, hash-linked, reproducible by an independent reviewer 90 days later.

The P-L-I-C-A methodology is formalised in SDB-26 — the first open benchmark for measuring synthetic document bypass rate in identity verification systems. Published under CC BY 4.0. github.com/sevrusik/SDB26 →

Common questions

Before you reach out.

Does Plica replace our KYC vendor?

No. Plica is an independent forensic layer on top of your existing stack. We do not replace Sumsub, Onfido, Veriff, or any other vendor. We audit what they produce and the pipeline that feeds them — independently.

What document types does Phase 0 cover?

Phase 0 covers JPEG, PNG, and WebP image files — passports, ID cards, utility bills, bank statements submitted as images. PDF and video analysis is available from Phase 1. No real PII at Phase 0 — use anonymised or synthetic labelled samples.

Do you sign NDA before Phase 1?

Yes. Phase 1 involving real client material requires a mutual NDA, a Data Processing Agreement, agreed retention limits, and subprocessor disclosure before any data transfer. We prepare standard templates. Phase 0 requires no NDA.

Who is the right contact at our company?

MLRO, Head of Compliance, Head of Fraud, or Chief Risk Officer. The conversation is most productive with someone who owns the KYC decision process and has visibility into vendor contracts and audit obligations.

Is this relevant if we already have a KYC vendor?

Especially then. The gap Plica addresses is not in the vendor — it is in the pipeline before the vendor, and in the evidence record after. Most vendors do not provide signal-level audit trails. That is not their product. It is ours.

What is SDB-26?
